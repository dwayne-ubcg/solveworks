**HQ:** Menlo Park/San Ramon, CA | **Founded:** 1948 | **Public:** NYSE: RHI

**Revenue:** $5.80B (2024) — down from ~$7.2B in 2022 peak. Lost ~20% of top line in 2 years.

**Specialties:**

- Marketing & Creative
- Legal (Robert Half Legal)

- 315 office locations worldwide

**Strengths:**
- Brand recognition is unmatched in finance/accounting
- 75+ years of client relationships
- Protiviti cross-sell creates stickiness
- Strong temp/contract book

**Weaknesses Rylem Can Exploit:**
- **Corporate bureaucracy** — 315 offices and 14,700 employees = slow decision-making, rigid pricing
- **Revenue decline** — lost $1.4B in top-line over 2 years, signaling client churn and market share loss. Clients are shopping alternatives.
- **Not diversity-certified** — no MBE/WOSB/diversity supplier status. Enterprise companies with supplier diversity mandates CANNOT count Robert Half spend toward diversity goals. Rylem wins those RFPs automatically.
- **Commodity feel** — clients report they feel like a number; high recruiter turnover means relationships break down
- **Premium pricing** — their scale means higher overhead passed to clients. Rylem can undercut on margin while maintaining quality.
- **Over-reliance on F&A** — if your role isn't accounting or technology, they're less sharp
- **Glassdoor complaints:** High-pressure sales culture internally, revolving door of recruiters = candidate experience suffers

**Rylem Pitch vs. Robert Half:**
> "Robert Half is a great brand name that you're paying a premium for. We're diversity-certified — which means your procurement team can count every dollar with us toward your supplier diversity goals — and you get a direct line to decision-makers who actually pick up the phone."

## TOP RYLEM EXPLOITATION OPPORTUNITIES

### 1. The Diversity Procurement Gap
Every enterprise and government client with a supplier diversity program NEEDS diversity-certified vendors. None of the Big 5 competitors can check that box. **Rylem's diversity certification is a direct revenue driver in any RFP that includes supplier diversity requirements.** Target procurement VPs and Chief Diversity Officers, not just HR.

### 2. Vendor Consolidation Play
Most enterprise clients are using TEKsystems (IT) + Aston Carter (Finance/HR) + Insight Global (engineering) + Robert Half (accounting) — 3-4 vendors doing what Rylem can do with one. Lead with: "How many staffing vendors are you managing? We can consolidate that — plus check your diversity box."

### 3. Kforce Client Poaching
Kforce is visibly shrinking (revenue declining ~17% from peak). Their clients are being underserved. Actively prospect Kforce clients in Finance + Technology verticals — they're already looking for alternatives.

### 4. Mid-Market Owners
TEKsystems and Aston Carter actively ignore companies under $500M in revenue. Robert Half is too expensive. Insight Global is too pushy. Mid-market companies ($50M-$500M) with 50-500 employees are underserved — this is Rylem's bread and butter.

### 5. Government Contract Angle
Diversity certification opens doors to government contract set-asides that none of the Big 5 can access. Local and state governments, federal agencies, and defense contractors all have diversity sourcing mandates. This is a whole market segment Rylem can own that competitors can't even enter.

### 6. The Relationship Quality Argument
All the big firms have high recruiter turnover (Insight Global and Robert Half especially). Clients are constantly re-onboarding new reps. Rylem's smaller size = account stability and continuity. Mike's 18-year tenure is a proof point.
