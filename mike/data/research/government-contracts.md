## 1. Priority Certifications to Pursue

### A. SAM.gov Registration (REQUIRED — Do This First)
**What it is:** System for Award Management — federal government's vendor database. You cannot receive any federal contract without being registered here.

- **URL:** sam.gov
- **Cost:** Free
- **Time:** ~1–2 weeks to activate
- **Required NAICS codes for staffing:**
  - `561320` — Temporary Help Services ⭐ Primary
  - `561311` — Employment Placement Agencies
  - `541612` — Human Resources Consulting Services
  - `561330` — Professional Employer Organizations (PEOs)
  - `541611` — Administrative Management & General Consulting
- **Action:** Register at sam.gov with UEI (Unique Entity Identifier), verify all NAICS codes are listed on your profile.

---

### B. MBE Certification — NMSDC (Highest Value Cert)
**What it is:** Minority Business Enterprise certification via the National Minority Supplier Development Council. This is the gold standard for corporate and government diversity supplier programs.

- **URL:** nmsdc.org/mbes/mbe-certification
- **Cost:** Varies by regional council (~$500–$1,500/year)
- **Time:** 60–90 days
- **Who certifies:** Local NMSDC Affiliate Council (in WA: Northwest Mountain Minority Supplier Development Council)
- **Key requirement:** 51%+ owned/controlled by a minority U.S. citizen (Asian, Black, Hispanic, Native American, etc.)
- **Value:**
  - Many Fortune 500s and government prime contractors **require** MBE subs
  - Federal agencies with diversity spend mandates actively seek MBEs
  - Unlocks NMSDC's corporate member network (500+ major corporations)
  - Huge advantage when teaming with large primes (they need your cert to meet their diversity goals)

---

### C. SBA 8(a) Business Development Program
**What it is:** 9-year federal contracting program for socially and economically disadvantaged small businesses. Arguably the most powerful government contracting certification available.

- **URL:** certifications.sba.gov
- **Cost:** Free
- **Time:** ~90 days for SBA to process once application is complete
- **Duration:** 9 years (4 developmental + 5 transitional) — **one lifetime use only**
- **Requirements:**
  - Small business (staffing NAICS 561320 size standard = $30M average annual receipts — Rylem qualifies)
  - 51%+ owned by socially/economically disadvantaged U.S. citizen
  - Owner personal net worth ≤ $850K, AGI ≤ $400K/year, assets ≤ $6.5M
  - In business ≥ 2 years ✅ (Rylem has 18 years)
- **Benefits:**
  - Sole-source contracts up to **$4.5M** (no competition required)
  - Competitive set-aside contracts
  - Access to SBA Mentor-Protégé Program
  - Joint venture eligibility with large primes
  - Free business development assistance from SBA

> ⚠️ **IMPORTANT:** 8(a) is one-time-only. Once used, you can never participate again. If Rylem qualifies, this should be evaluated carefully — 9 years of preferential access to federal contracts is enormous, but timing matters.

---

### D. WOSB / EDWOSB (Women-Owned Small Business)
**What it is:** SBA certification that reserves certain federal contracts for women-owned businesses. 5% of all federal contracting dollars are mandated for WOSBs.

- **URL:** certifications.sba.gov
- **Cost:** Free (via SBA) or ~$300–$600 via third-party certifiers
- **Requirements:** 51%+ owned/controlled by women who are U.S. citizens; women manage day-to-day operations
- **Note:** Applies only if Rylem is majority women-owned. Confirm ownership structure.

---

### E. DBE Certification (Disadvantaged Business Enterprise)
**What it is:** Used for federally-funded transportation, DOT, and infrastructure projects. Different from MBE — administered at state level.

- **Certifying body in WA:** Washington State Office of Minority & Women's Business Enterprises (OMWBE) — omwbe.wa.gov
- **Cost:** Free
- **Applies to:** Airport contracts, transit agencies (Sound Transit, Metro), DOT projects, port authorities
- **Staffing angle:** Airports, transit agencies, and port authorities hire staffing firms — DBE certification gives preferred access

---

### F. HUBZone Certification
**What it is:** Historically Underutilized Business Zone — if Rylem's principal office is in a qualifying census tract, they get preferential treatment + 10% price evaluation preference in open competitions.

- **Check eligibility:** maps.certify.sba.gov/hubzone/map
- **Note:** 35% of employees must reside in a HUBZone — harder for staffing firms. Evaluate carefully.

## 2. Key Federal Contracting Vehicles for Staffing

### A. GSA Multiple Award Schedule (MAS) — Professional Services
**What it is:** The federal government's primary procurement channel. A GSA Schedule contract lets you sell directly to any federal agency without going through a full competitive bid each time.

- **Relevant Schedule:** MAS — Professional Services (formerly Schedule 736 for Temporary Admin/Professional Staffing)
- **Current consolidated schedule SIN (Special Item Number) for staffing:**
  - **SIN 561320** — Temporary Help Services
  - **SIN 541612** — HR Consulting
- **How to apply:** GSA eOffer system at gsaadvantage.gov
- **Time to award:** 3–6 months
- **Annual reporting:** Must report sales to GSA quarterly; pay 0.75% Industrial Funding Fee
- **Value:** Once on schedule, agencies can buy from you on a task-order basis. Massive time-saver for buyers — many agencies **only** buy from schedule holders.
- **Revenue potential:** Mid-size staffing firms on MAS schedule generate $1–10M+/year in federal task orders

---

### B. HCaTS — Human Capital and Training Solutions
**What it is:** GSA's governmentwide acquisition contract (GWAC) specifically for human capital, HR, training, and talent management services. The premium vehicle for staffing-adjacent federal work.

- **Two pools:**
  - **HCaTS** — Unrestricted (large businesses)
  - **HCaTS SB** — Small Business (set aside for small businesses ✅)
- **Scope:** HR consulting, workforce planning, organizational development, executive coaching, training, staffing/talent acquisition support
- **Status:** HCaTS is currently in a recompete cycle. Watch for new solicitation announcements at sam.gov.
- **Why it matters:** Agencies with HCaTS access are mandated to use it for HR/staffing work above certain thresholds — getting on this vehicle = preferred vendor status government-wide.

---

### C. OASIS SB (One Acquisition Solution for Integrated Services — Small Business)
**What it is:** GSA's GWAC for complex, multi-disciplinary professional services. Not purely staffing, but includes workforce management and HR consulting components.

- **Note:** OASIS+ is the current successor contract. SB pool is available.
- **Relevance:** Good for larger consulting-type staffing engagements with federal agencies.

## 4. Teaming with Large Primes

### Why This Matters for Rylem
Large government contractors (Booz Allen, SAIC, Leidos, Accenture Federal, ManpowerGroup Government, etc.) **need** certified diverse small businesses as subcontractors. Federal law requires prime contractors to have subcontracting plans that include small/diverse businesses. An 8(a) or MBE-certified Rylem becomes a very attractive teaming partner.

### How to Get in Front of Primes

1. **SBA Mentor-Protégé Program** — Get formally paired with an established large prime. Benefits:
   - Joint venture as "small business" on set-aside contracts
   - Up to 6-year agreement
   - Mentor provides business development help, BD introductions, past performance credentials
   - Apply at certify.sba.gov (must come with an identified mentor)

2. **Subcontracting Opportunities:**
   - Search **SUB-Net** (SBA's subcontracting opportunities database): sba.gov/sub-net
   - Large primes post here when they need small/diverse subs for existing contracts
   - Target: DoD, DHS, VA, HHS, OPM (all major staffing buyers)

3. **GSA Subcontracting Directory:**
   - Large prime contractors post their small business subcontracting plans
   - Reach out to their Small Business Liaison Officers (SBLOs)

4. **Target Prime Contractors in Staffing:**
   - ManpowerGroup Government Solutions (already on MAS schedule)
   - Staffmark Government Solutions
   - Accenture Federal Services (HR transformation work)
   - Booz Allen Hamilton (workforce consulting)
   - SAIC (IT staffing needs)
   - IBM Federal (tech talent)

### Joint Venture Approach
Under SBA rules, an 8(a)-certified Rylem can form a joint venture with a large prime and compete as a small business for 8(a) set-aside contracts. This is how many small staffing firms win large federal contracts — the prime brings past performance and capacity; Rylem brings the certification and staffing expertise.

## 6. State & Local Government Opportunities

Don't overlook state/local — often faster to win and less competitive than federal.

### Washington State
- **OMWBE** (omwbe.wa.gov) — certifies MBE/WBE for state contracts
- **DES Contracts** (des.wa.gov) — Department of Enterprise Services manages state-level staffing contracts
- **Sound Transit, Port of Seattle, King County Metro** — all have diversity spend goals and buy staffing

### Nationwide State Programs
- Many states have their own MBE/DBE certification programs that qualify you for state contracts
- California, Texas, New York, Florida have massive state staffing contract spend
- Target states where Rylem already places talent
