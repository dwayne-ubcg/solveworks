# Candidate Recycling Playbook — Rylem Staffing
**Created:** 2026-03-10  
**Owner:** Rylem AI (for Mike Dades, CEO)  
**Scope:** 100,000+ dormant candidates across 18 years of database history

---

## Executive Summary

Rylem's 100K candidate database is the single most underutilized asset in the business. These aren't cold leads — they're people who already raised their hand, went through some form of screening, and trusted Rylem enough to share their information. Re-engaging even 5% of that database represents 5,000 warm conversations. At Rylem's average placement fee, recycling dormant candidates is one of the highest-ROI activities the team can run.

This playbook turns that dormant database into an active revenue channel.

## Part 1: Database Segmentation

Before any outreach, the database needs to be sliced into actionable buckets. Don't blast everyone — segment by relevance, recency, and fit.

### Tier 1: Hot Warm (0–24 months dormant)
- Last activity: 2024–2026
- These candidates are likely still in the workforce and remember Rylem
- Priority: **Immediate re-engagement**
- Expected response rate: 20–35%

### Tier 2: Warm (2–5 years dormant)
- Last activity: 2021–2023
- Career has probably progressed — title/salary expectations may have changed
- Priority: **High — career progression angle**
- Expected response rate: 10–20%

### Tier 3: Cold (5–10 years dormant)
- Last activity: 2016–2020
- Significant career drift possible; verify they're still in the workforce
- Priority: **Medium — use alumni/reconnect angle**
- Expected response rate: 5–12%

### Tier 4: Archive (10–18 years dormant)
- Last activity: pre-2016
- Many may have retired, changed industries, or moved on
- Priority: **Low — targeted only for senior/executive placements**
- Expected response rate: 2–6%

### Segmentation Variables (pull from CEIPAL)
Run each tier through these filters to create sub-segments:

| Variable | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **Skill/Vertical** | IT, Finance, Marketing, Creative, HR, Admin — match to current open reqs |
| **Location** | City/state — match to client geography |
| **Last Placed vs. Never Placed** | Placed candidates = proven performers, higher priority |
| **Salary Range (last known)** | Avoid wasted outreach if expectations don't match current market |
| **Reason for Last Status** | "Not available" vs. "declined offer" vs. "placed elsewhere" — different messaging |
| **Specialty/Certifications** | CPA, PMP, AWS cert, etc. — high-value niche targeting |

### Quick Win Segments to Run First
1. **IT candidates, Tiers 1–2, placed at least once** — highest placement probability
2. **Finance/Accounting, Tiers 1–2, CPAs or Controllers** — perennial demand
3. **Any candidate tagged to a currently active client** — immediate match potential
4. **Senior candidates (Director+) in any vertical, Tier 1–3** — lower volume, higher fee

## Part 2: Re-Engagement Email Templates

### General Rules
- Subject lines under 50 characters
- First line must reference something real (their field, past work with Rylem, market conditions)
- One CTA only — keep it simple
- No attachments on first touch
- Always include unsubscribe option (CAN-SPAM compliance)

## Part 3: Compliance & Legal

**This section is non-negotiable.** Improper outreach to dormant candidates can create legal exposure. Follow these rules.

### CAN-SPAM Act (Email)
- **Every email must include:** Rylem's physical mailing address, a working unsubscribe link
- **Honor opt-outs within 10 business days** (federal requirement)
- **No deceptive subject lines** — subject must reflect the email content
- **Never use purchased lists** without verifying opt-in status

### GDPR (If candidates are EU-based)
- Even if Rylem is US-based, any EU candidate contact requires a **lawful basis** (legitimate interest or explicit consent)
- Document the lawful basis for outreach
- Honor data deletion requests ("right to be forgotten") within 30 days
- If unsure: skip EU candidates or consult legal counsel

### CCPA (California)
- California candidates have the right to know what data Rylem holds and request deletion
- Must have a "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" disclosure in outreach
- Maintain a log of deletion requests

### Staffing Industry-Specific
- **Do not contact candidates currently employed by a Rylem client** without client consent — this can violate non-solicitation clauses
- **Do not promise specific compensation** until a client offer is confirmed
- **Candidate data must be secured** — do not export full lists to personal spreadsheets or unsecured drives

### Data Hygiene Before Outreach
- **Run email list through an email verification tool** (ZeroBounce, NeverBounce, or similar) before sending — reduces bounce rate and protects sender reputation
- **Suppress anyone who previously unsubscribed** — pull suppression list from CEIPAL before every campaign
- **Remove candidates who explicitly declined future contact** — document this in CEIPAL notes

### Recommended: Build a Suppression List in CEIPAL
Create a custom tag: `DO_NOT_CONTACT` — add anyone who:
- Unsubscribed from email
- Requested data deletion
- Expressed frustration with previous outreach
- Is currently placed and under a non-solicit period

## Part 4: Metrics & KPIs

Track these weekly. If numbers fall below floor, adjust messaging or segment.

### Email Campaign Metrics

| Metric | Target | Floor (Investigate Below) |
|---|---|---|
| Open Rate | 25–40% | <15% |
| Reply Rate | 8–15% | <4% |
| Bounce Rate | <3% | >5% (clean list immediately) |
| Unsubscribe Rate | <0.5% | >1% (messaging problem) |
| Spam Complaints | <0.1% | >0.3% (stop campaign) |

### Pipeline Metrics (Recruiter-Level)

| Metric | Weekly Target |
|---|---|
| Candidates re-engaged (replied) | 15–25 |
| Candidates reactivated (re-entered pipeline) | 5–10 |
| Candidates submitted to clients | 3–6 |
| Placements from recycled candidates | 1–2/month |

### Business Impact Metrics (Monthly)

| Metric | How to Track |
|---|---|
| Revenue from recycled placements | Tag placements in CEIPAL as "Recycled - [Tier]" |
| Time-to-fill (recycled vs. new candidates) | Compare fill time — recycled should be 20–40% faster |
| Cost per placement (recycled vs. sourced new) | Recycled = near zero sourcing cost |
| Database coverage | % of Tier 1/2 contacted within 30 days |

### Dashboard to Build (Feeds into solveworks.io/mike/)
Track in `memory-recent.json` and `dashboard.json`:
- Total candidates re-engaged this week
- Candidates moved to active pipeline
- Recycled candidate placements this month

## Part 7: Recruiter Execution Guide

### Daily Recycling Workflow (Per Recruiter)
1. **Morning:** Review replies from recycling campaigns — respond within 2 hours
2. **Midday:** Log all reactivated candidates in CEIPAL — tag as `Recycled - Tier [X]`
3. **Afternoon:** Match reactivated candidates to open reqs — submit same day when possible
4. **EOD:** Log any call attempts from recycling list — note outcome in CEIPAL

### How to Handle Common Responses

**"I'm not looking right now"**
> "Totally understand — would it be okay if I kept you in mind for the right opportunity? What would make you take a call — is it the role, the comp, the flexibility, or all three?"
> → Tag in CEIPAL: `Passive - Revisit [timeframe]`

**"I left [industry] / changed careers"**
> "Thanks for letting me know — really appreciate the update. If you ever want to reconnect with [industry], we're always here. Good luck with everything."
> → Update CEIPAL record with new industry/status; flag for annual check-in only

**"How did you get my info?"**
> "You worked with Rylem back in [year] — you shared your information with us when you were exploring opportunities. We've kept your profile in our system. Happy to remove you if you'd prefer — just say the word."
> → If they want removal: immediately tag DO_NOT_CONTACT, remove from all campaigns, document in CEIPAL

**"I'm interested — tell me more"**
> → Treat like a fresh active candidate: qualification call within 24 hours, update all profile info, match to open reqs immediately

### Sheila's KPI Add-On
On top of the 20 screens/week standard KPI, add:
- **10 recycling touches per week** (calls or emails to Tier 1–2 non-responders)
- **5 recycled candidates reactivated per month**

## Part 8: Candidate Recycling for Client Development

This is the angle most staffing firms miss. Dormant candidates aren't just future placements — they're future clients.

### When a Candidate Becomes a Lead
If ZoomInfo or LinkedIn enrichment shows a re-engaged candidate has moved into a **hiring manager or people leader role**, immediately:
1. Flag them in CEIPAL as both Candidate AND Prospect
2. Alert the BDM — this is a warm intro to a new account
3. Adjust the outreach: shift from candidate pitch to relationship pitch

**Example:**
- 2019: Mike's candidate was an IT Project Manager
- 2026: ZoomInfo shows they're now VP of Technology at a 500-person company
- **This isn't just a candidate — this is a $200K+ account opportunity**

### Script for Candidate-to-Client Pivot
> "Congrats on the VP role — that's a big move. I wanted to reach out not just to check in on your own career, but to see if Rylem might be able to help your team. We do a lot of work placing senior IT talent nationwide, and I'd love to buy you 20 minutes to catch up and see if there's a fit."
