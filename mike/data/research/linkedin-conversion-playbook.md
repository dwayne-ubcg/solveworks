# LinkedIn Conversion Playbook for Rylem Staffing
*Generated: March 9, 2026 (overnight research)*
*Situation: 150K company page followers, daily posting for 1 year, ZERO deals generated*

---

## THE CORE PROBLEM

150K followers + daily posting + zero deals = **content that engages but doesn't convert.**

This is the most common mistake on LinkedIn: treating it like a branding exercise when it should be a **lead generation machine.** The gap between "people see your content" and "people call you to buy" requires a deliberate conversion strategy — not just more content.

---

## PART 1: CONTENT STRATEGY THAT DRIVES LEADS (Not Just Engagement)

### The Lead-Generating Content Framework

Every post Mike writes should map to one of 4 categories. The ratio matters:

| Category | % of Posts | Purpose | Example |
|----------|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Authority** | 30% | Establish Mike as the expert | Industry insights, market data, 30-year perspective |
| **Proof** | 25% | Show Rylem delivers | Results, retention stats, client success stories (anonymized) |
| **Problem-Aware** | 30% | Surface pain points buyers have | "Why your staffing partner ghosted you after placement" |
| **Direct CTA** | 15% | Ask for the meeting | "If you're hiring in [vertical], let's talk this week" |

### What's Wrong With the Current Content
ChatGPT-generated company page content typically falls into two traps:
1. **Generic inspiration** — "Great teams start with great people!" (nobody calls you after reading this)
2. **Company bragging** — "We're proud to announce..." (prospects don't care about your announcements)

### What Converts Instead
Posts that make the reader think: **"This person understands MY problem better than I do."**

#### High-Converting Post Types:

**1. The Contrarian Take**
> "Most companies evaluate staffing firms on fill time and bill rate. Both matter. Neither predicts success. Here's what actually does..."

Why it works: Challenges conventional thinking → reader feels smarter → associates Mike with insight → follows/engages → eventually calls.

**2. The Specific Number**
> "We've had zero unwanted contractor attrition in 7 years across hundreds of placements. Here's exactly how we did it."

Why it works: Specific numbers stop the scroll. Abstract claims don't.

**3. The "If This Is You" Post**
> "If you're a VP of HR at a company with 500+ employees and your staffing partner disappeared after the last placement, you're not alone. Here's what good looks like..."

Why it works: Directly addresses the target buyer. They feel seen. They DM.

**4. The Client Story (Anonymized)**
> "A client came to us after burning through 3 staffing firms in 2 years. We placed 6 contractors. All 6 stayed. Here's what we did differently..."

Why it works: Proof, not claims. Story format is compelling. Reader imagines their own situation.

**5. The Market Intelligence Post**
> "Block just laid off 4,000 employees. If you're hiring in fintech, compliance, or payments — there's a window right now to get talent you couldn't afford 6 months ago."

Why it works: Timely, useful information that decision-makers can act on. Positions Mike as someone who's plugged in.

### Posts That DON'T Generate Leads (Stop Doing These)
- "Happy [Holiday]! From our team to yours" ❌
- Reshared articles with no commentary ❌
- Generic motivational quotes ❌
- "We're hiring!" posts (attracts candidates, not clients) ❌
- Team photos with "great culture" captions ❌
- Any post that could come from any staffing firm ❌

## PART 2: LINKEDIN SALES NAVIGATOR — Best Practices for Staffing

### The Setup (Do Once)

**1. Build Saved Searches for Ideal Buyers:**
- Search 1: VP of HR OR Head of Talent OR VP of People → Companies 200-5000 employees → US → Industries: Technology, Financial Services, Healthcare
- Search 2: VP of Procurement OR Supplier Diversity → Fortune 500 → US
- Search 3: Hiring Managers → Title contains "Director" → Companies that posted 10+ jobs in last 30 days

**2. Set Up Lead Alerts:**
- Get notified when saved leads change jobs (new VP of HR at a company = fresh relationship opportunity)
- Get notified when target companies post news or have leadership changes
- Get notified when saved leads post content (engage with their content before pitching)

**3. Build Account Lists:**
- List 1: "Active Clients" — current billing clients (for expansion)
- List 2: "Dormant Clients" — companies Rylem placed at before (re-engagement)
- List 3: "Net New Targets" — from our prospect research files
- List 4: "Recently Funded" — from funded-companies.md
- List 5: "Diversity Program Targets" — from diversity-opportunities.md

### The Daily Workflow (15-20 min/day)

**Morning (8:00-8:15 AM):**
1. Check Sales Navigator alerts — any lead changes, news, or job posts?
2. Review "Who viewed your profile" — these are warm leads
3. Send 3-5 connection requests to targeted prospects with personalized notes
4. Engage (like/comment) on 3-5 posts from target buyers

**Rules for Connection Requests:**
- ALWAYS personalize the note (max 300 chars)
- Reference something specific: their company, a post they wrote, a shared connection
- Never pitch in the connection request. Ever.
- Template: "Hi [Name], I noticed [specific thing]. I lead a staffing firm that works with companies like [their company]. Would love to connect."

**Rules for Engagement:**
- Comment with substance, not "Great post!" 
- Add your perspective or a data point
- Ask a thoughtful question
- This puts Mike's name (and CEO title) in front of the prospect without pitching

### The Conversion Sequence (After Connection)

**Day 1:** Send connection request (personalized, no pitch)
**Day 3-5:** If accepted, engage with their recent content (like + comment)
**Day 7-10:** Send a value-first message:
> "Hi [Name], I've been following what [Company] is doing in [area]. Curious — are you seeing the same tightening in the [IT/Finance/Marketing] contractor market that we're seeing with our clients? Happy to share what we're observing."

**Day 14-21:** If they respond, continue the conversation. If not, send one more value touch:
> "Thought you might find this interesting — [share a relevant market insight, article, or data point]. No agenda, just thought of you."

**Day 30:** If still no response, add to long-term nurture list. Engage with their content periodically. Try again in 90 days.

**If they respond at any point:** Move to a phone call. Goal is ALWAYS a 15-minute conversation, not a LinkedIn sale.

## PART 3: TURNING FOLLOWERS INTO BOOKED MEETINGS

### The Funnel: Follower → Engaged → Connected → Conversation → Meeting

**Step 1: Follower → Engaged**
- Post 3x/week from Mike's personal account
- Use the content types from Part 1
- End EVERY post with a hook that invites response (question, poll, "agree or disagree?")
- Respond to EVERY comment within 2 hours (algorithm rewards this)

**Step 2: Engaged → Connected**
- When someone likes or comments on your post, check their profile
- If they're a target buyer (VP of HR, hiring manager, procurement), send a connection request
- Reference their comment: "Appreciate your perspective on my post about [topic]. Would love to connect."
- This converts 40-60% (vs. 15-20% for cold requests)

**Step 3: Connected → Conversation**
- Use the Sales Navigator sequence from Part 2
- Never pitch in DMs. EVER. Lead with value, insight, curiosity.
- Ask questions about THEIR business, not your services
- The goal: get them talking about their hiring challenges

**Step 4: Conversation → Meeting**
- When they mention a hiring need, pain point, or frustration → offer to help:
> "That sounds like something we've solved for other companies in your space. Worth a 15-minute call? I can share what's worked."
- Propose a SPECIFIC time: "How about Tuesday at 2pm?"
- Remove friction: "Just a quick call — no pitch, just exploring if there's a fit."

### Key Metrics to Track
- **Profile views per week** — leading indicator of content performance
- **Connection acceptance rate** — should be 30%+ with personalized requests
- **DM response rate** — should be 20%+ with value-first approach
- **Meetings booked per month** — the ONLY metric that matters

### Target: 4 meetings per month from LinkedIn within 90 days

## PART 4: LEAD-GENERATING vs. VANITY METRIC POSTS

### Vanity Metrics (Feels Good, Doesn't Convert)
| Metric | Why It's Misleading |
|--------|-------------------|
| Likes | Recruiters and job seekers like everything. Doesn't mean buyers saw it. |
| Impressions | Seeing ≠ reading ≠ caring ≠ calling |
| Follower count | 150K followers, zero deals. QED. |
| Comments like "Great post!" | Engagement farming, not buyer interest |

### Lead Indicators (Actually Matters)
| Metric | Why It Matters |
|--------|---------------|
| Profile views from target titles (VP, Director, CHRO) | They're checking you out |
| Connection requests FROM prospects | They want to connect with YOU |
| DMs asking questions about staffing | Direct buying signal |
| Comments with substance from decision-makers | They're engaging with your ideas |
| "Who viewed your profile" spikes after a post | Content is reaching the right people |

### How to Tell If a Post Generated Leads
After EVERY post, check:
1. Who viewed your profile in the 48 hours after posting?
2. Any new connection requests from target titles?
3. Any DMs?
4. Any comments from people at target companies?

If none of the above → the post engaged the wrong audience. Adjust.

## PART 5: COMPETITOR ANALYSIS — Top Staffing Firms on LinkedIn

### What the Big Firms Do (And Where They Fail)

**Robert Half (1.8M followers)**
- Heavy corporate content, employer branding, salary surveys
- Strength: Salary guides are genuinely useful content (referenced industry-wide)
- Weakness: Feels corporate, impersonal. CEO rarely posts personally.
- Rylem advantage: Mike's personal voice is more authentic. Decision-makers trust people over brands.

**Insight Global (400K+ followers)**
- Culture-heavy content — team events, awards, charity
- Strength: Strong employer brand for recruiting
- Weakness: Doesn't speak to CLIENTS at all. 100% candidate/employee focused.
- Rylem advantage: Mike can speak directly to buyers (VPs, CHROs) — a lane Insight Global ignores.

**Kforce (200K+ followers)**
- Mix of market insights, case studies, thought leadership
- Strength: Good use of data and market intelligence in posts
- Weakness: Still feels corporate. No personal voice.
- Rylem advantage: Mike + 30 years experience + personal storytelling > corporate content

**Hays Recruiting (3M+ followers)**
- Global firm with strong content machine
- Strength: Regular salary surveys, market reports, infographics
- Weakness: So big it's generic. Doesn't feel local or specialized.
- Rylem advantage: Specialist in 6 verticals, local Seattle presence, diversity-certified

### The Gap Rylem Can Own
No major staffing firm has a CEO consistently posting personal, opinionated content that speaks directly to hiring managers and CHROs. The big firms post corporate content. The small firms don't post at all. **Mike's personal voice in the CEO lane is a wide-open opportunity.**
