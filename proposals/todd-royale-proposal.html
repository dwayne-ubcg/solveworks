The Estimating Problem

What commercial flooring estimating actually looks like — and where it breaks down

Royale Floors & Surfaces has been installing commercial flooring across Atlantic Canada for over 30 years. Hospitals, schools, government buildings — the kind of projects where a single building can have eight different flooring types across dozens of zones. VCT in the hallways, porcelain in the lobbies, epoxy in the kitchens, rubber in the gyms, resilient sheet in the patient rooms. Every zone has different materials, different square footage, different compliance requirements.

Right now, estimating a major hospital project means manually reviewing architectural drawings, measuring each zone, looking up material quantities, applying your supplier pricing and markup, checking spec compliance for healthcare environments, and assembling a professional bid document for the GC. That process takes 2-3 days per project. When you're busy — and you are, you're actively hiring — those days are the bottleneck between an RFP landing on your desk and a bid going out the door.

In commercial flooring, the first quality bid back often wins. Speed isn't just efficiency — it's revenue.

Questions & Answers

Common Questions

Straight answers — no pitch

📐 "How accurate are the blueprint measurements?"

The agent reads architectural PDFs and calculates area measurements from scaled drawings. It's not replacing your experienced eye — you review every estimate before it goes out. But it eliminates the manual measuring and math that takes hours. Think of it as a very fast junior estimator that does the grunt work while you verify the numbers.

🔧 "Can it handle our specific supplier pricing and product lines?"

Yes. During onboarding we load your actual supplier pricing, product catalogs, and markup rules. When prices change, you tell the agent and it updates. Every estimate uses your real numbers, not generic industry averages.

🏥 "Does it actually know healthcare flooring compliance?"

It flags known requirements — antimicrobial ratings, slip resistance standards, fire code adhesive specs, infection control zones. It doesn't replace your knowledge of local building codes, but it catches the specs that are easy to miss when you're moving fast on a multi-zone estimate. You've seen the standards a thousand times. The agent makes sure nothing slips through.

⏱️ "How long until we're up and running?"

Three to four weeks from kickoff. Week one is loading your pricing, product catalog, and typical project structures. Weeks two and three we build and test with a real project you've already completed — so you can verify accuracy against known numbers. Week four is live deployment with hands-on support.

📱 "Do I need to learn new software?"

No. You text your agent via Telegram — same as texting anyone on your crew. Send a floor plan, get an estimate back. Approve it, get a bid document. The dashboard is a single web page you can check from your phone or office computer. No training courses. No login credentials to forget.

🚪 "What if we cancel?"

No long-term contracts. Month-to-month. If you cancel, automations stop and you keep the Mac Mini hardware. Your pricing data and project history are always yours. We'd rather earn your business every month than lock you into something you can't exit.
