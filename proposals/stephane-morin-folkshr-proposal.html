The BDR Problem

What's actually happening when your reps aren't on the phone

Folks HR has a strong BDR team. Three outbound reps who know how to have conversations, build rapport, and convert pipeline. You've built a bilingual operation covering Quebec and Ontario. You have 1,200 customers and a product that genuinely solves HR admin for Canadian SMBs.

The problem isn't your reps. The problem is what the system forces them to do. Every two months, your whole industry rotation resets. Someone decides it's time to move from construction to manufacturing. That means manually filtering Apollo, scraping new contacts, verifying emails and phone numbers one by one, rebuilding sequences from scratch, and reloading LinkedIn outreach — which is now fully manual since Apollo killed the auto-connect feature. Human nature takes over: reps default to the easier tasks and avoid the phone, which is their highest-leverage activity.

Meanwhile, the partnership ICP (HR consulting firms under 50 employees in Ontario) is nearly impossible to filter cleanly — "HR" catches engineering firms, computing companies, unrelated businesses. Your partnership manager spends hours sorting false positives.
