# AI Workflow Architecture Workshop — Run-of-Show

**Duration:** 4 hours (240 minutes)  
**Format:** Virtual (Zoom/Google Meet) or In-Person  
**Participants:** Business owner + 1-3 key team members (cap at 5)  
**Facilitator:** SolveWorks

---

## Pre-Workshop Checklist

### Client Prep (Send 1 week before)

Email subject: **"Prep for Your AI Workflow Architecture Workshop"**

Request the following from the client:

- [ ] **Tool inventory** — List every software tool they use (CRM, email, calendar, accounting, project management, communication, etc.)
- [ ] **Team roster** — Who's attending, their roles, and what they're responsible for day-to-day
- [ ] **Top 5 time sinks** — The 5 tasks/activities that eat the most time or cause the most frustration
- [ ] **Current org chart** — Even informal: who reports to whom, who handles what
- [ ] **Sample workflows** — 2-3 examples of how a typical task moves through the business (e.g., "lead comes in → we do X → then Y → then Z")
- [ ] **Access to observe** — Invite us to any shared tools (read-only) we can review beforehand

### SolveWorks Prep (Internal)

- [ ] **Research the business** — Website, social media, Google reviews, industry context
- [ ] **Review their tool stack** — Understand integration possibilities before the session
- [ ] **Pre-fill templates** — Add client name, date, known tools to all 4 deliverable templates
- [ ] **Test video/screenshare** — If virtual, ensure smooth connection
- [ ] **Prepare whiteboard** — Miro/FigJam board (virtual) or physical whiteboard (in-person)
- [ ] **Print/share templates** — Have all 4 deliverable templates ready for collaborative editing

## Workshop Agenda

### Block 1: Business Deep-Dive (60 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand how the business actually operates — not the org chart version, the real version.

**0:00 – 0:10 | Welcome & Context Setting**
- Introductions (if needed)
- Set expectations: "This is a working session, not a presentation. We're here to understand your business deeply and build you a concrete plan."
- Explain deliverables they'll walk away with
- Ground rule: "There are no dumb answers. The messier the truth, the better the plan."

**0:10 – 0:30 | Business Overview**

Key questions to ask:
- "Walk me through a typical day. Start from when you wake up."
- "What's the first thing you check? What apps do you open?"
- "Who are your customers? How do they find you?"
- "What does your sales process look like end-to-end?"
- "How do you currently handle [their industry's core workflow]?"
- "What's the thing that keeps falling through the cracks?"
- "If you could clone yourself, what would the clone do all day?"

**0:30 – 0:45 | Pain Point Excavation**

Dig deeper:
- "You mentioned [X] — walk me through the last time that happened."
- "How many hours a week do you spend on [admin/email/scheduling]?"
- "What's the most annoying part of your day?"
- "What would you do with 10 extra hours a week?"
- "Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"

**0:45 – 1:00 | Team Perspective**

For each team member present:
- "What's your role? What do you actually spend your time on?"
- "What's the most repetitive thing you do?"
- "Where do things get stuck waiting for someone else?"
- "What information do you wish you had faster?"

**Facilitator Notes:**
- Listen for workarounds — "Oh, we just use a spreadsheet for that" = automation opportunity
- Note emotional reactions — frustration signals high-value problems
- Capture exact language — use their words in deliverables
- Start filling the Process Map template during this block

---

### Block 2: Process Mapping (60 minutes)

**Goal:** Map 3-5 core workflows visually. Identify every bottleneck, handoff, and time sink.

**1:00 – 1:10 | Identify Core Workflows**

"Based on what we just discussed, here are the workflows I want to map:" [List 3-5]

Typical workflows to map:
- Lead → Customer (sales process)
- New customer → Onboarded
- Service request → Delivered
- Invoice → Payment collected
- Daily operations / morning routine
- [Industry-specific workflow]

**1:10 – 1:50 | Map Each Workflow**

For each workflow (10-12 min each):

1. **Trigger:** "What kicks this off? A phone call? An email? A form?"
2. **Steps:** "Then what happens? And then? And then?" (Keep asking until complete)
3. **Who:** "Who does each step? Is it always the same person?"
4. **Tools:** "What tool are you in when you do this step?"
5. **Time:** "How long does this step take? Minutes? Hours? Days?"
6. **Failures:** "Where does this break down? What goes wrong?"
7. **Workarounds:** "Do you have any hacks or shortcuts for this?"

**Fill in the Process Map template live on screen/whiteboard.**

**1:50 – 2:00 | Time Allocation Summary**

Review the time allocation table:
- "Looking at everything we've mapped, you're spending roughly [X] hours/week on [category]. Does that feel right?"
- Validate numbers with the team
- Identify total recoverable hours

**BREAK (10 minutes) — 2:00 to 2:10**

---

### Block 3: Architecture Design (60 minutes)

**Goal:** Design the AI system — what's automated, what stays human, how it all connects.

**2:10 – 2:25 | Automation Opportunity Scoring**

Pull up the Automation Scorecard template.

For each workflow mapped in Block 2:
- Score ROI, Feasibility, Simplicity, Speed to Value
- Do this collaboratively: "On a 1-5 scale, how much time would automating [X] save?"
- Rank and prioritize together
- Separate into 🟢 Do First / 🟡 Do Next / 🔴 Later tiers

**2:25 – 2:50 | Architecture Blueprint**

For the top 3-5 automation opportunities:
- Draw the workflow architecture: trigger → AI steps → human checkpoints → output
- Define confidence thresholds: "If the AI is less than [X]% sure, it escalates to you"
- Map escalation channels: "Urgent = Telegram. Non-urgent = email digest."
- Identify integrations needed: which tools connect to what

Key questions:
- "For [workflow], would you trust AI to handle this fully, or do you want to review first?"
- "What's the worst thing that could happen if AI got this wrong?"
- "How quickly do you need to know if something needs your attention?"

**2:50 – 3:10 | Context Engineering Plan**

Map what business knowledge needs to be encoded:
- Products/services, pricing, policies
- Customer segments, common scenarios
- Brand voice, communication style
- Industry-specific knowledge
- "What do new employees need to learn in their first month? That's what the AI needs too."

Identify context gaps:
- "Is this documented anywhere, or is it in someone's head?"
- "If [person] left tomorrow, what knowledge would walk out the door?"

---

### Block 4: Roadmap & Priorities (50 minutes + Wrap)

**Goal:** Build the 90-day plan with costs, milestones, and success metrics.

**3:10 – 3:30 | Phase Planning**

Using the Roadmap Template:

**Phase 1 (Days 1-30):**
- "Which quick wins should go live first?" [Pick top 2-3 from scorecard]
- Define success metrics for each
- Identify setup requirements (hardware, access, accounts)

**Phase 2 (Days 31-60):**
- "Which core workflows go live next?"
- Define integration priorities
- Set escalation rules and thresholds

**Phase 3 (Days 61-90):**
- "What advanced capabilities matter most?"
- Optimization targets
- Expansion opportunities

**3:30 – 3:45 | Investment & ROI**

Walk through costs openly:
- Workshop investment: $[amount] (already paid)
- System build: $2,500 setup (workshop fee applies as credit if they proceed)
- Ongoing: $250/mo managed service
- Hardware: ~$600-900 (Mac Mini, client provides)
- AI model costs: ~$30-100/mo (transparent passthrough)

ROI calculation:
- "Based on our scoring, the system saves you roughly [X] hours/week"
- "At $[their hourly value], that's $[amount]/year in recovered capacity"
- "Payback period: [X] months"

**3:45 – 4:00 | Wrap-Up & Next Steps**

- Recap the 4 deliverables and when they'll receive final versions (48 hours)
- Ask: "What surprised you most today?"
- Ask: "Based on what we mapped, how are you feeling about moving forward?"
- If interested in building: outline immediate next steps
- If not ready: "No pressure. The blueprint is yours. When you're ready, we're here."
- Thank everyone for their time

## Facilitator Tips

1. **Listen more than you talk.** This is about understanding their business, not showing off AI knowledge.
2. **Use their language.** If they say "leads" not "prospects," use "leads" in all deliverables.
3. **Get specific.** "A lot of time" isn't useful. "About 3 hours every Tuesday" is.
4. **Validate with the team.** The owner thinks the process works one way. The team knows how it really works.
5. **Don't oversell.** If something isn't a good candidate for automation, say so. Trust is more valuable than scope.
6. **Capture quick wins.** Even before the system is built, they should walk away with 2-3 things they can improve this week.
7. **Energy management.** The break at the midpoint is non-negotiable. People fade after 2 hours of deep thinking.
8. **Document everything.** Record the session (with permission). You'll catch things you missed live.
